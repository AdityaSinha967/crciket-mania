The Three Lions: Pioneers of Modern Cricket

Overview & History

The England Cricket Team, governed by the England and Wales Cricket Board (ECB), is a pivotal force in international cricket. As the birthplace of the game, England holds a unique place in cricket history, competing in the first-ever Test match in 1877.

In recent years, England has revolutionized limited-overs cricket with an aggressive "Bazball" approach in Tests and powerful batting in white-ball formats, leading to significant success in global tournaments.

Leadership

  • Captain (Tests): Ben Stokes
  • Captain (ODIs & T20Is): Jos Buttler
  • Head Coach (Red-Ball): Brendon McCullum
  • Head Coach (White-Ball): Matthew Mott
